1. Summary

The word "hippopotamus (or hippos)" means "river horse". The hippopotamus is a mammal in the family Hippopotamus. It is one of the most representative animals in Africa. However, the hippopotamuses are endangered animals. They live in high humidity. They spend an average of 18 hours a day in the water, and come out of the water to eat grass at night. It is considered one of the most aggressive and violent animals in Africa, and the hippopotamus' front teeth can cause fatal injuries.

2. Distribution

Hippos live mainly in sub-Saharan Africa. It used to live in other parts of North Africa and other continents, but it is now extinct because the temperature and climate do not match. In addition, the hippopotamus population is still decreasing throughout the continent. This is because illegal hunting is increasing in order to sell hippopotamus fangs on behalf of elephants' ivory in black markets in Africa.

According to a 2008 survey, hippos now have 126,000 to 149,000 populations.

3. Appearance

3-1. Features

The hippopotamus has portrayed as cute animals in many animations thanks to their large heads and cute little ears. However, the hippopotamus has a scary hidden figure. The hippopotamus' mouths can open up to 150 degrees. [2]

Also, the hippopotamus has a great length of fangs, which are as long as 60 centimeters. It is twice as long as the fangs of the Tyrannosaurus. Two-thirds of the hippopotamus' fangs are located inside the jawbone, and one-third can be seen from the mouth. Finally, the nostrils of hippopotamus are also developed to open and close easily in the water.

The hippopotamus has greyish-brown skin on its back and light pink skin on its belly. It has less hair throughout its body and only a little hair on its mouth, ears, and tail. The hippopotamus' four legs have webbed membranes developed between its toes so that it can swim in the water.

4. Lifestyle

An average of 20 to 30 hippos lives in groups.

Hippos are very susceptible to ultraviolet light due to their skin characteristics compared to other animals. The hippopotamus can be badly hurt by only 18 hours of exposure to the sunlight. In some cases, hippos die from ultraviolet rays after only three days of exposure to sunlight.

Therefore, hippos secrete red liquid, not blood, from their skin, which is similar to sweat, which protects the skin of hippos that are weak against ultraviolet rays. It also helps to maintain the body temperature of hippos. Most hippopotamuses' main habitat is Africa, where the sun is high in the jungle, so it lives almost underwater during the day and protects it from ultraviolet rays and keeps cool.

[5]

5. Caracteristic

Hippos, unlike they look, have tremendous characteristics. First, hippos are physically developed to live long in the water. The hippopotamus' ears, eyes, and nostrils are located on the highest part of the hippopotamus' body, so it can live in a state of immersion in the water. And when hippos swim in the water, they can close their ears and nostrils to block the water.

The hippopotamus' bodies are also dense, allowing them to walk and swim in the water. Hippos have short legs, but the speed of swimming in the water is fast enough to follow a motorboat.[6] In addition, the speed of running on land is also fast. Although the hippos' body is large, they can run up to 45 km/h.

Hippos reach adulthood at the age of seven, weighing more than 1,200 kilograms and running at speeds of nearly 50 kilometers per hour. From this point on, the only animals that can compete against the hippopotamus one-on-one are homogeneous and elephants.

The hippopotamus has a huge length of fangs and a powerful jaw.[7] Their jaw strength is about a ton. And hippos have very thick leather with thick layers of fat. Their wound resilience is also very fast, with severe wounds recovering within 10 minutes.

Hippos also have good hearing, so it does not matter to eat grass at night. Hippos put pressure on other animals with their low, ringing cries.[8]

Such a powerful and scary hippo is an animal that should never be touched because it is an animal that shows violence not only to other animals but also to humans.
